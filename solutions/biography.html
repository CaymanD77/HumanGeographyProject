Biography: Stéphane Roche

Stéphane Roche

Stéphane Roche is a Professor of Geographical Information Science at Laval University in Quebec, Canada. His research focuses on the role of spatial data, geographic information systems (GIS), and urban planning in creating more sustainable and effective smart cities.

Roche has spent much of his career exploring how technology can enhance decision-making, improve urban management, and encourage public participation in city governance. His academic background includes work in geospatial science and urban geography, where he has contributed a number of publications addressing how smart cities must integrate spatial data to function effectively.

His work helps to highlight the importance of citizen engagement, advocating for a model where residents can more actively contribute to city planning through digital tools, acting as citizen sensors. Roche has collaborated with international organizations to promote spatial enablement strategies that improve urban management in real-world scenarios.

His research has been published in geography journals such as Progress in Human Geography and GeoJournal.
